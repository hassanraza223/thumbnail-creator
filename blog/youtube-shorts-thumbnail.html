Shorts Thumbnail vs Regular Video Thumbnail

The main difference between a Shorts thumbnail and a regular video thumbnail is the aspect ratio YouTube uses to display them. Regular videos use a 16:9 (landscape) frame. Shorts use a 9:16 (vertical/portrait) frame inside the app.

However, YouTube stores both as the same horizontal JPG format on its CDN servers. This means when you download a Shorts thumbnail, you will get the full horizontal image — which often includes more context around the face or scene than you see in the vertical Shorts player view.
